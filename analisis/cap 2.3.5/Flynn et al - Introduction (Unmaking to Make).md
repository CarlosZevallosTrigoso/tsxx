## PROPIAS DE ESTA FUENTE

### P1 · Qué se propone el volumen (pasaje entero)

(p. 4) — anuncio directo:
> «Our aim with this book therefore is to reorient the theoretical horizon back towards Latin America, paying specific attention to arts practitioners working within contexts of coloniality.»

(pp. 3–4) — razón de existencia:
> «Such concerns are entirely valid. And yet, when we as editors began to conceive of this project in 2020, we felt there was a need to offer a re- joinder to the increasingly superficial and problematic forms of engage- ment with decolonisation occurring in North American and European art spaces. Over recent years, that conviction has only grown as the production of the discourse of decolonisation has become ever more monopolised by the institutions of the Global North – whether pertaining to the arts or academia – and such discourse's relationship to artistic prac- tice has shifted from one of theoretical inspiration to mere illustration. One of our central contentions in this book is that artistic practice… cannot be reduced to simply making visible and legible theories produced by the academy.»

(pp. 6–7) — configuración/propósito editorial:
> «We envisage this collection therefore as an open- access repository, not as an attempt to gain space, but rather as a form of interlocution, a starting point for further conversations. The editorial intention of this publication lies squarely in its configuration as a research tool, aimed at fostering and consolidating a robust and specialised body of literature on Latin American art, thus contributing to the advancement of academic research in this area.»

### P2 · «Unmaking» (explicación del título y operación que nombra)

El cuerpo del texto **NO enuncia ni glosa literalmente la palabra «Unmaking» ni la frase «Unmaking to Make»** (NO LO DICE). El único lugar donde «unmaking» aparece es el **título del propio texto**: «Introduction: Artistic practice and **the unmaking of coloniality**» (p. 1). No hay pasaje que explique el título del volumen.

Lo más cercano a nombrar esa operación (paráfrasis: sin usar «unmaking»):
- (p. 4) «this interlinked capacity to both theorise how to resist and dismantle the aims of colonial regimes, as well as to actively seek to bring this about».
- (pp. 4–5) «the fundamental and pioneering role of art in theorising, modelling and enacting plural modes of dismantling colonial regimes».
- (p. 4) «proposing a radical rereading of the colonial imposition».

(Marcado como dato faltante conforme a la regla 4: no hay glosa del término «unmaking».)

### P3 · El autoexamen (examinarse, revisarse, reconsiderarse, deshacerse a sí mismo / a la institución que publica)

- (p. 4) «In conceptualising this volume, however, we were also aware of the importance of speaking to a context without reifying it. While we were increasingly dismayed by institutional attempts to co- opt the discourse of decolonisation before returning to business as usual, we were clear that the focus of this volume should not be on critiquing such pretences and thereby centring them all over again.»
- (p. 6) «We are aware that the presence of the perspectives we articu- late in this book may be considered merely a passing phenomenon by the wider art world and international curatorial sphere, just like anything else arising from the logics of capitalism. And we understand that there is a long history of how coloniality has appropriated the theoretical work of those it seeks to contain.»
- (p. 7) «In this manner, we have reflected care- fully on the advantages and disadvantages of initiating a conversation be- tween Anglophone academia and Portuguese and Spanish speaking arts practitioners. We recognise that creating such a volume is to work within asymmetrical power relations and yet, we hope that by acknowledging that which is contested, we can bring together actors from very different spheres of practice.»
- Sobre las instituciones (no sobre la editorial del volumen): (pp. 8–9) el examen crítico de la propia teoría decolonial y (pp. 9–10) las críticas de Cusicanqui, Cumes, Adusei-Poku, Makaran/Gaussens al Grupo MCD. Nota: es autoexamen del *campo/discurso*, no de la editorial que publica el volumen.

**Aviso de población:** el texto distingue con cuidado entre la crítica institucional que rechaza («box to be checked», co-optación) y su propia posición. No confundir a los artistas latinoamericanos del volumen con «el artista crítico» institucional del tramo: el texto afirma que «of the artists contributing to this book, none have worked with the decolonial framework in a longitudinal sense» (p. 10).

### P7 · CONTRAEVIDENCIA (el volumen presentado como resolución / cierre / superación, o «ya hicimos lo que había que hacer»)

Nota general: el texto se autodefine mayoritariamente como **apertura** («starting point», «open-access repository», «form of interlocution», «starting point for further conversations»), lo cual **refuerza** la tesis del tramo (el autoexamen no clausura) y por eso está listado en P6. Abajo transcribo los pasajes que sí tiran hacia lo contrario — donde el volumen se atribuye un logro, una superación o un salir del atolladero de la co-optación — que es lo que P7 pide. Marco con **[CONTRADICE]** los que chocan con «el autoexamen no clausura la disputa sino que le da escenario / la institución exhibe su autoexamen como prueba de vigencia».

**[CONTRADICE]** (p. 4) — el volumen afirma haber salido del bucle de criticar-a-la-institución, en lugar de darle escenario:
> «While we were increasingly dismayed by institutional attempts to co- opt the discourse of decolonisation before returning to business as usual, we were clear that the focus of this volume should not be on critiquing such pretences and thereby centring them all over again. Instead, we sought to base the volume and its contributions on epistemological positions that exist outside the North American and European canon and are often rendered invisible by that corpus of know- ledge.»
> `[reconstruido]`: «…the focus of this volume should not be on critiquing such pretences and thereby centring them all over again. Instead, we sought to base the volume and its contributions on epistemological positions that exist outside the North American and European canon and are often rendered invisible by that corpus of knowledge.»
> (Se presenta como superación: no critica la pretensión institucional sino que se sitúa «fuera» del canon; declara ubicarse en un afuera genuino.)

**[CONTRADICE]** (p. 3–4) — reclamo de logro / transformación efectiva del canon:
> «While much scholarly work details how artistic projects are informed by, or otherwise illustrate, decolonisation as a pro- ject, we work to manifest how Latin American artistic practice theorises and articulates plural modes of being that are dynamically reshaping the canon.»
> (Afirma que la práctica ya está «reshaping the canon» — logro en curso, no disputa abierta.)

**[CONTRADICE]** (p. 10) — se coloca «más allá» de las constricciones institucionales/académicas como resultado:
> «By contrast, what we articulate in this volume is how the generative potential of open- ended and experimental art work goes beyond such narrow constraints, resulting in the theorisation and articu- lation of complex and unpredictable modes of being.»
> (El «By contrast… goes beyond… resulting in» presenta al volumen como superación de las limitaciones que acaba de describir.)

**[CONTRADICE]** (p. 5) — reclamo demostrativo (el volumen «demuestra»):
> «Through a series of chapters that bring together scholars, critics, artists, curators and activists, we dem- onstrate how artistic practice from across Latin America has shaped the decolonial turn that so characterises contemporary scholarly thought, and how by extension, such practice points to future directions of de- parture.»
> (Verbo «demonstrate» + «has shaped»: se atribuye probar un hecho consumado, no abrir una disputa.)

Candidato más débil (lo dejo señalado sin marcar CONTRADICE porque va acompañado de reservas en la misma frase): (p. 6) «As practitioners ourselves, we have not only thought deeply about such questions, but also maintained an embodied commitment to them» — reclamo de «commitment» cumplido, matizado luego por «may be considered merely a passing phenomenon».
