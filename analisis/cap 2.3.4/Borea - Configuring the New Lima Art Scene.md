### B3 · El argumento en sus propias frases (tres pasajes enteros)

**(a) Donde anuncia qué va a hacer** [ancla page-13/page-14, primera frase "This book is about the formation…", línea 193]:
> "This book is about the formation of the contemporary art scene in Peru's capital and its mechanisms of articulation over the last 20 years. It argues that the transformation of Lima's art scene needs to be traced in relation to Peru's social renewal following the end of Fujimori's regime; to Lima's neoliberal transformation and recasting as a creative city; and to the strengthening of Latin American art networks and changes in the larger art world. It is at this intersection of political economy and transnational articulations that the book locates its analysis. … Through in-depth examination of a specific case, this book contributes to show the ways in which two projects, 'the global art world' and 'the creative city', closely articulate and fuel a particular organisation of the arts."

**(b) Donde enuncia su tesis** [ancla page-14/page-15, primera frase "By identifying these three moments…", línea 197]:
> "By identifying these three moments that can be summarised as of artistic and curatorial explorations; of institutionalisation and regional strengthening, of market growth and market-driven logics, this book illuminates the changing notions and practices of the local, the global, the social and indigeneity … I show how contemporary art and art agents enabled an arena for intercultural agendas during the first half of the 2000s when art was not on the city's radar, becoming gradually a means of urban gentrification and a vehicle for art agents' transnational participation in international art venues and networks of power. I show the mechanisms by which these agents fuel Lima as an art node and promote Peruvian art abroad, and conclude that the strengthening of Lima's art scene and its articulation into larger art circuits has fostered not the spread of new art values and forms but rather the connection and expansion of dominant art views and prototypes through grounded articulations mobilised by transnational elite actors. Moreover, I argue that we are witnessing the overlap, friction and assemblage of large programmes such as the 'global art world' and 'the creative city' in the management of art, both of which fuel market logics and engage with leading standards and values."

**(c) Donde cierra** [Conclusion, ancla page-239, primera frase del párrafo final "I have shown in this book that new connections…", línea 1644]:
> "I have shown in this book that new connections and geographies of art do not mean new art values, protocols or epistemologies *per se*. The articulation with the larger art world requires the 'emerging' art worlds to recalibrate their ways of working, talking, showing and creating value in art, and therefore to remove from the public eye local battles that have worked in the reconfiguration of specific art scenes. … What we are witnessing is less about 'the global' and the multiplicity, and more about the expansion and connection of the upper class power (Harvey 2006) in different parts of the globe – usually dominant groups linked to Western forms and neoliberalising projects, and who in Latin America tend to be of European descent … The art map has expanded: Latin American art, Peruvian art and the Lima art scene are now 'on the map,' but it is still a transnational white elite urban map, despite the narratives on the political and indigeneity that many of the Latin American art scenes forge today."

**MALI, subastas y el límite mercado/museo** [page-138/139, primera frase "The launch of the MALI Summer Auction and Party…", línea 968]:
> "The launch of the MALI Summer Auction and Party, which takes place at exclusive beach clubs, was the third strategy. … Auctions in Lima constitute a 'community of the privileged' (Braudillard 1981: 117), although not precisely as tournaments of value, as disputed bids rarely occur, but as events of reinforcing an urban upper class in which being at the auction and the after-party is what matters. The MALI's auctions challenge the traditional boundary between the art market and the museum that Olav Velthuis (2007) explains…"

**Precios/comisiones de galerías** [page-211, línea 1462]:
> "Since its launch, to ensure the vigorous promotion and expansion of its artists' markets on an international scale Revolver has represented a small number of artists via the notion of representation and splitting profits 50/50. Both of these mechanisms … were unusual in Lima in 2008, where galleries operated mainly locally with a large group of artists, and sporadic international work, splitting their profits 35/65 per cent in favour of the artist."

**Ventas de Pinta (cifras)** [page-185, línea 1283]:
> "In 2009, the fair brought together 34 galleries, invoiced \$8 million, and received 7,000 visitors at its locations in the Metropolitan Pavilion and the Altman Building. In 2010, Pinta was held at Pier 92, where the Armory Show is held, with 54 galleries, sales of over \$10 million, and 12,000 visitors."

**Premios/bancos que se retiraron** [page-208, línea 1446]:
> "throughout the 1990s the Lima art scene comprised various diverse exhibition/selling spaces, from art galleries to restaurants with spaces for exhibition. Banks ran cultural programmes in their exhibition rooms and numerous awards invigorated the small, local but dynamic Lima art scene. However, by the turn of the 2000s, with political conflict over the restoration of democracy and especially the economic downturn many cultural-market venues and awards were withdrawn."

### P8 · CONTRAEVIDENCIA (¿dice que la falta de infraestructura IMPIDE / FRENA / VUELVE IMPOSIBLE la circulación?)

**RESULTADO: NO se halló ningún pasaje donde Borea afirme que la falta/incompletitud de infraestructura impida, frene o vuelva imposible la circulación.** No hay **[CONTRADICE]**.

Al contrario, la formulación de Borea es consistentemente compatible con la tesis del tramo de CZT: la infraestructura débil/incompleta es el **terreno o condición** dentro del cual la validación y la circulación **igual ocurren** (por vía de individuos, curadores, coleccionistas, ferias-prototipo, colaboraciones peer-to-peer). Los pasajes que un lector adversarial podría querer usar como contraevidencia, y por qué NO lo son:

1. **"institutions were significantly weak" (L711)** — no dice que ello impida la validación; dice lo opuesto: "The validation of contemporary art … was provided **not by institutions** … **but by the iterations of two curators**." La circulación se produce *a pesar de* la debilidad institucional. → **[APOYA la tesis del tramo]**, no la contradice.

2. **"biennials are cancelled while the art scene grows" (L974)** — construcción explícitamente concesiva: la escena *crece* mientras las bienales se cancelan. → **[APOYA]**.

3. **"art museums and the art market platform were still weak" (L627)** — encuadrado como el marco donde "contemporary art developed". → **[APOYA]**.

4. **Límite de la participación internacional (L1231): "such joint projects are generally held in museums and institutions that already have the infrastructure"** — este es el pasaje MÁS cercano a una tesis de "sin infraestructura no circulas". Lo transcribo entero por si CZT quiere discutirlo, PERO nótese que Borea lo aplica a las **grandes colaboraciones peer-to-peer** y añade que el MALI **sí** tiene esa infraestructura y **sí** participa; no afirma imposibilidad de circulación, sino jerarquía y selectividad dentro de una circulación que existe:
> "such joint projects are generally held in museums and institutions that already have the infrastructure and the administrative 'efficiency', such as the Museo de Arte de Lima in Peru, and the leading art venues' trust to embark on international collaborative projects." [L1231]

5. **"the frames and forms through which articulations happen and circulations take place are too narrow" (Conclusion, L1644)** — habla de *estrechez/selectividad* de las formas de circulación (a quién deja fuera), no de *ausencia* de circulación por falta de infraestructura. → no es contraevidencia P8.

*Paráfrasis del extractor (señalada, sin interpretar más allá de lo literal): donde CZT esperaría que Borea dijera "la falta de infraestructura impide la circulación", Borea dice reiteradamente que la circulación y la validación se producen igual, gestionadas por individuos y por plataformas-prototipo, sobre una infraestructura que apenas existe. La discrepancia entre ambos NO está en el dato, sino en el signo evaluativo: Borea lee esto críticamente (como expansión de lógicas de mercado y poder de élite), CZT lo lee como prueba de que la criticidad ya es manejable. Ninguno de los dos afirma imposibilidad. No marco [CONTRADICE] porque no lo hay.*
